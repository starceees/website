Raghuram Cauligi Srinivas

I'm a graduate research assistant in the Agile Robotics and Perception Lab (ARPL) at NYU, where I build autonomy and perception pipelines for quadrotor drones as part of my M.S. in Electrical Engineering (expected May 2025).

My work spans visual–inertial odometry, dense mapping and outdoor localization on the NVIDIA Jetson platform. Previously I developed large language model evaluation and retrieval systems at Scoutos Inc. and contributed to drone localization research at RBCCPS, IISc.

MXene (Ti3C2T x)/TMD (ReSe2) nanohybrid-based flexible electromechanical sensors for cervical collar strain and shoulder load detection applications
Vivek Adepu, Manav Tathacharya, Raghuram CS, Venkat Mattela, Parikshit Sahatiya, Raghuram Cauligi Srinivas
Journal of Micromechanics and Microengineering, Year
pdf

Heavy backpacks cause chronic neck pain, a concern not fully addressed due to costly, complex alert systems. We developed low-cost ReSe₂/Ti₃C₂Tx nanohybrid flexible sensors, integrating them into wearable strain and load detection systems. The sensors offer high sensitivity, fast response, durability over thousands of cycles, and enable wireless data transfer to smartphones, opening new prospects in flexible electronics.
